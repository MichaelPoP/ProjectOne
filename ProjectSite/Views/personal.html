POETRY

'I Wonder'

I wonder,

How we all fit together and fall apart

I wonder,

Where we all are and where we all have been


But if we knew would it help us understand?


I wonder,

Why we pick one over the other

I wonder,

When will we choose


But if we knew would it help us decide?


I wonder,

What I am

I wonder,

Who I am


But if I knew would I still know myself?


I wonder,

If we knew,

What would we think to do?


I wonder,

If we could,

Would we think we should?


I wonder,

If I am

Who would give a damn?

READING

John Rawls - A Theory of Justice Thomas Malthus - An Essay on the Principle of Population David Ricardo - On The Principles of Political Economy, and Taxation
These are some of the most famous works by my favorite thinkers. They are philosophers, political economists, scientists and statiticians who all had a passion for thinking about how people interact with one another on the most base level. None of these works are a 'light' read however they all have particular sections you can read in a few hours that give amazing insight into the human condition.
Adam Smith - The Wealth of Nations Alexander Bickel - The Least Dangerous Branch Robert Nozick - Anarchy, State, Utopia
